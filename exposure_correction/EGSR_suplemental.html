Color Clipping and Over-exposure Correction
for Videos and Still Images

Supplemental Materials

The dynamic range of the real world cannot always be captured by conventional cameras. Irradiance values beyond the capacity of the camera sensors are clipped, leading to over-exposed images or videos. Further image processing, like gamut mapping, might also clip color information beyond the gamut boundary of the intended device. Irrespective of the cause, color clipping and over-exposure lead to loss of texture and detail, changing the color appearance and visual quality of the image, and potentially impacting further image processing. We propose a new over-exposure and clipping correction method for images and videos, which relies on the correlation between RGB channels of color images to recover clipped information. Using a novel segmentation approach we treat clipped regions coherently both spatially and temporally. Over-exposed areas with no information are reconstructed according to surrounding colors, maintaining the appearance of highlights, while boosting the local brightness. Our method is evaluated using objective metrics as well as a subjective study based on an ITU standardized protocol, showing that our correction leads to significantly better results than previous similar methods. Additionally, we show that our method is applicable as a general image and video enhancement algorithm or as a preprocessing step for several image processing algorithms including inverse tone mapping and feature detection.
